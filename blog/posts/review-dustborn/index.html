[Review] Dustborn

It has now been five years since Quantic Dream jumped aboard the publishing train with Spotlight. Since then, it has published games like Under the Waves, Lysfanga, and the Sea of Solitude: Director’s Cut for Nintendo Switch. Its next release, developed by Red Thread Games, is about to land on PC and on Sony and Microsoft’s eighth- and ninth-generation consoles. Dustborn throws you into a hilarious narrative action-adventure with a comic-book aesthetic, where a diverse group of friends, under the cover of a music band, must embark on a dangerous road trip across the United States to deliver a package of great importance.

The power of words

In the universe of Dustborn, not only do ordinary humans coexist, but also anomalous people. These individuals, although human, share one trait: they possess special powers when using words. Our protagonist, Pax, is an exile and con artist who can wield words as weapons. The correct use of these words not only determines the fate of the characters and their relationships, but can also be used to defeat enemies, solve puzzles, or overcome obstacles.

As the leader of the Dustborn crew, Pax must command her squad, making the most of her companions’ abilities in every situation. There is more than one way to solve many challenges, but the decisions you make will affect the timeline, shaping the narrative outcome.

Narrative under the Quantic Dream seal

Quantic Dream has proven its mastery of narrative with major titles like Heavy Rain, Beyond: Two Souls, and Detroit: Become Human, and Dustborn is no different. Red Thread Games has crafted a narrative adventure with an interesting, entertaining plot and a cast of charismatic characters. The narrative design offers content for every type of player, whether you're only interested in the main story or want to dive deeper into character relationships and subplots. It also includes several technical innovations that add depth and realism to conversations and allow the narrative experience to be tailored to the player’s taste.

  • Live dialogues: At certain moments during conversations, the player is warned when the dialogue is about to shift topics. You must respond quickly or you may lose your chance to intervene. This new feature makes the dialogue feel alive and realistic, as characters do not stand around waiting for the player’s response.
  • Dynamic conversational perspective: During most conversations, players can choose the camera angle. This allows for different views of the same conversation and lets you observe all characters’ facial expressions. Even so, the developers have given extra weight to the main shots so that players who prefer it can experience the game as the Norwegian studio originally designed it.

4-in-1: Narrative, exploration, rhythm and combat

Dustborn brings together a wide array of mechanics from different genres, and does it very well. Branching narrative, world exploration, rhythm minigames, and third-person combat blend into a cohesive title built on strong storytelling foundations. Without a doubt, the highlight of Dustborn’s exploration is ME'EM, a portable console that accompanies you throughout your journey. A fun detail is that it can be taken out during conversations, and Pax will display it in real time on screen.

Dustborn awaits you

The launch of Dustborn is just around the corner, and Pax and her eccentric friends are waiting for you to help them cross the great American landscape. Save the date, August 20, and enjoy this comedic 11-chapter adventure. Remember that if you preorder the game before release, you’ll receive the “Vision Tour” bundle, which grants aesthetic digital modifiers for Pax.

If you’re a fan of physical editions, Dustborn includes a physical release that will absolutely satisfy you. This edition is available for PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and Xbox One, and contains:

  • Two postcards of key locations visited by the crew.
  • A map of the American Republic, listing all the different stops the crew must make before reaching their final destination.
  • A 32-page prequel comic about the crew’s life before accepting this risky mission.
  • Stickers featuring iconic elements from the game.
